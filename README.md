## Materials for MSBA Math & Coding Workshop

The informal math & coding workshop will provide a brief review/introduction of some tools and concepts from coding, mathematics, and statistics. The workshop will focus on some essentials that will help you succeed in the MSBA program. The goal is not to master these topics in two days but rather to give you some recent exposure to them in preparation for the program. Day 1 will focus on programming concepts and tools, and Day 2 will focus on mathematics and statistics topics, though there will be a mix of topics between days.

Here are the workshop materials. Tentative schedule, subject to change:

- Day 1 Morning
  - Introduction
  - Colab/Jupyter
  - Python
  - Data wrangling in Python (Pandas)
  - Data visualization
- Day 1 Afternoon
  - Setting up your workspace
  - Installing: Anaconda, Quarto, Positron, Git
  - Working with Quarto and Positron
  - Version control/collaboration and Git/Github
- Day 2 Morning
  - Logarithms
  - Calculus: Derivatives
  - Calculcus: Integrals
- Day 2 Afternoon
  - Statistics (regression)
  - Matrix algebra

The workshop will involve many hands-on activities, including computing activities. You should bring a laptop computer to the workshop that you can install programs on. Chromebooks, iPads, and some very old model laptops will not be sufficient to install Python. If this requirement is limiting for you, please contact me asap.

I encourage you to attend the workshop in person if possible. There will be plenty of time to work on activities, ask questions of me, and meet and work with your fellow students. Even if you are comfortable with much of this material, I encourage you to attend the workshop to help out your fellow students! Also, free lunch! However, the workshop is optional. I encourage you to review the workshop materials and make your decision.

The workshop will not be livestreamed or recorded, but I will hold Zoom check-in times both days at 11:00, 12:00, 2:00, 2:45; each check-in time will be about 10 minutes. Even if you are not attending in person, I encourage you to work on the materials following the schedule above and Zoom into the check-in times to ask questions.
